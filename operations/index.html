Section 1

The Guest Journey

From first click to post-stay follow-up. Every touchpoint is designed, automated where possible, and personal where it matters. AI handles logistics so the team can focus on hospitality.

Pre-Arrival (Booking to Check-In)

Online Booking
Guest books via website or phone. Selects dome, dates, and optional add-on packages (therapy, IV drips, meal plan). Square processes payment — 50% deposit at booking, balance due 7 days before arrival.
Confirmation & Intake
Automated email: booking confirmation, property guide PDF, dietary restriction form, liability waiver (SignWell), and intake questionnaire for any clinical add-ons. All digital, all mobile-friendly.
7 Days Before
Balance charged automatically. Welcome email with packing suggestions, what to expect, directions, and a personal note from the ranch manager. Any incomplete forms trigger a reminder SMS.
24 Hours Before
SMS with unique smart lock code (activates at 3pm check-in), WiFi password, property map, parking instructions, and emergency contacts. Guest's dome is prepped and confirmed ready.

Arrival Through Departure

Self Check-In (3pm)
Guest enters dome via smart lock code. Welcome basket on the nightstand: water, tea, local snacks, handwritten welcome card, printed week schedule. Ranch manager available for a personal greeting — not required, but offered.
Evening Orientation
Casual group gathering at the fire circle or communal dining room. Property walkthrough, safety briefing, weekly schedule overview, introductions. Sets the tone: warm, grounded, unhurried.
During Stay
Daily schedule accessible via SMS or printed card. Meals at set times. Add-on services booked through the front desk or app. Mid-stay check-in from ranch manager on Day 3-4: "How's everything? Anything you need?"
Checkout (11am)
Day-before SMS reminder with checkout steps. Guest completes digital condition report. Smart lock code deactivates. System dispatches housekeeping. Post-checkout: feedback survey + integration resources email.

The 3 Moments That Matter Most

Hospitality research is clear: guests judge their entire stay on arrival first impression, the lowest point (any problem, however small), and the final moment (checkout/goodbye). Nail the welcome basket, respond instantly to any issue, and send them off with a genuine farewell + a follow-up gift (discount code, branded item, or handwritten note mailed 1 week later).

Phased Approach

Phase 1 (Launch): Curated meal delivery partner + stocked pantry + private chef for dinners 3x/week. No commercial kitchen needed. ~$15,000/mo at avg occupancy.

Phase 2 (6-12 months): Private chef 5x/week for dinners. Breakfast/lunch stay self-serve. Kitchen assistant for daily prep. ~$18,000-$22,000/mo.

Phase 3 (Year 2+): On-site commercial kitchen, dedicated cook, full meal program included in all packages. Revenue opportunity: cooking classes, juice bar. Buildout: $15K-$30K.

Pricing Strategy: Build It Into the Rate

Don't charge for meals separately — include a $50/night "Nourishment Fee" in the room rate. At 72% occupancy that's $6,570/mo in food revenue against $13K-$18K in food cost. The gap is made up by therapy/add-on margin. Guests perceive "all-inclusive meals" as premium, not an extra charge. It's a booking differentiator.

Section 4

Housekeeping & Turnover

Turnover speed = revenue. A dome sitting empty and dirty is a dome not earning money. Target: 3-hour full turnover from checkout to next-guest ready.

Guest Checkout Checklist

Printed in-dome + sent via SMS the morning of checkout

  • ! Remove all personal belongings — room, bathroom, outdoor areas
  • ! Place used towels and linens in the laundry basket by the door
  • ~ Rinse any used dishes, place in sink or dish bin
  • ~ Dispose of trash in provided bins (separate recycling)
  • ! Turn off all lights, fans, AC/heater
  • ! Close and latch all windows and main door
  • ~ Return borrowed items (blankets, books, yoga mats) to welcome shelf
  • ! Check safe and under bed for forgotten items
  • ~ Complete the digital checkout form (condition report + feedback)

Staff Turnover Checklist

2-person team per dome · Target: 3 hours · Quality walkthrough before "Ready" status

  • ! Strip all linens — bed, pillowcases, mattress protector
  • ! Fresh linens — hospital corners, decorative pillows staged
  • ! Deep clean bathroom — toilet, shower, sink, mirror, floor
  • ! Restock: 2 bath towels, 2 hand towels, 2 washcloths, toiletries, TP
  • ! Vacuum/sweep all floors, mop hard surfaces
  • ! Wipe all surfaces — nightstands, desk, shelves, switches, handles
  • ! Restock welcome basket — water, tea, snacks, welcome card
  • ! Test HVAC, smart lock, WiFi, all lights
  • ! Inspect for damage — log and report to ranch manager
  • ! Spot check: open every drawer, check under bed, behind bathroom door
  • ✓ Mark dome as "Ready" in system — triggers next guest SMS notification

Automated Turnover Workflow

11:00am — Checkout
Smart lock code deactivates. System marks dome as "Departed." If guest hasn't completed digital checkout by 11:15am, ranch manager gets an alert.
11:15am — Housekeeping Dispatched
Automated SMS to cleaning team with dome assignment, any special notes (damage, extra mess, late checkout). Cleaning supplies pre-staged in the housekeeping closet.
11:30am - 2:00pm — Full Turnover
2-person team executes the full checklist. Deep clean, linen change, amenity restock, quality walkthrough. Ranch manager does final spot-check on any flagged units.
2:00pm — "Ready" Confirmed
Housekeeping marks dome as ready in the system. Automated SMS to incoming guest: "Your dome is ready! Your access code will activate at 3pm. See you soon."
3:00pm — Check-In Opens
Smart lock code activates. Guest arrives, enters, finds a perfect space. No front desk needed.

Linen & Laundry

At 18 beds, that's 36 sheet sets + 108 towels in rotation minimum (3x inventory: one on the bed, one in the wash, one in reserve). Option A: Commercial laundry service — pickup/delivery 3x/week, $600-$1,200/mo. Option B: On-site commercial washer/dryer ($3,000-$5,000 equipment) + housekeeping handles it. Break-even at 8 months. Go with Option A at launch, switch when volume justifies.

The "Nothing to Do" Problem

Retreat guests have two modes: overscheduled and bored. The schedule above fills every slot but marks afternoons as "Free Time" — this is intentional. Section 8

Property Operations

12 acres of Texas hill country with 10+ structures requires active maintenance year-round. Deferred maintenance compounds fast — fix it the day it breaks.

🌡

HVAC

6 domes + 3 yurts + retreat house = 10 HVAC systems. Quarterly servicing. Filter changes monthly May-Sep. Summer failure = guest refund + lost revenue. Budget $800-$1,500/mo maintenance.

💧

Water & Septic

Well pump or municipal hookup for 18+ guests daily. Septic system sized for 25+ capacity. Pump every 2-3 years ($400-$600). Grease trap if commercial kitchen. Water heaters per dome.

🌳

Grounds

Mowing, trail maintenance, tree trimming, fire mitigation (30ft defensible space around structures). Irrigation for garden/landscaping. Pest control monthly (fire ants, scorpions, snakes).

🏊

Pool & Plunge

Daily chemical testing and balancing. Weekly cleaning. Commercial pool permit required. Certified Pool Operator (CPO) on staff or contracted. Liability signage. Budget $400-$800/mo.

🔌

Electrical & Internet

10+ structures need reliable power. Generator backup for essential systems (well pump, smart locks, security). Starlink primary + cellular backup. Mesh WiFi nodes per dome. Budget $200-$400/mo.

🛡

Security

Cameras on common areas (never private spaces). Motion-activated trail lighting. Smart lock audit logs. Fire/smoke detection in every structure. Emergency call stations at pool and trailheads.

Seasonal Threats

Summer (Jun-Aug): 100+ degree days. HVAC is your biggest expense and risk. Schedule outdoor activities before 10am or after 6pm. Winter (Dec-Feb): Ice storms can shut property access for 2-3 days (see Feb 2021). Generator, pipe insulation, and guest communication plans critical. Year-round: Wildfire risk in drought conditions. Maintain defensible space, keep fire extinguishers in every structure, have evacuation route posted in every dome.

Licensing & Compliance

Two compliance tracks: hospitality (permits, taxes, safety) and clinical (DEA, medical board, HIPAA). Both must be airtight before opening day.

Hospitality Compliance

  • ! Short-Term Rental (STR) permit — City of Austin or Travis County
  • ! Hotel Occupancy Tax (HOT) — 6% state + local, collected per booking
  • ! Business license / DBA registration
  • ! Fire safety inspection — each dome and yurt
  • ! Commercial pool permit + CPO certification
  • ! Septic capacity permit for guest volume
  • ! Food handler certifications (kitchen staff)
  • ~ Commercial kitchen license (Phase 3)
  • ~ ADA accessibility (at least 1 dome + paths)
  • ! Workers' compensation insurance
  • ! Background checks — all staff with guest access

Clinical Compliance

  • ! DEA Schedule III registration (medical director)
  • ! Texas Medical Board supervision agreement
  • ! Licensed pharmacy relationship (ketamine sourcing)
  • ! Informed consent forms (attorney-reviewed)
  • ! HIPAA compliance — EHR, BAAs with all vendors
  • ! Emergency protocols — crash cart, AED on-site
  • ! Clinical SOPs documented + staff-trained
  • ! Professional liability / malpractice insurance
  • ~ Voluntary accreditation (JCAHO or similar)
  • ! Drug testing + EMR documentation protocols
  • ! Quarterly compliance audits
